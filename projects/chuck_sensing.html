Drill Rig Chuck Direction, Rotation, and Movement Sensing

Role: Electronics Design + Firmware + Validation Testing

Why does this project matter?

During drill operations, the chuck is repeatedly used to make and break rods. In these steps, rotation direction and RPM are safety-critical: an incorrect direction or uncontrolled rotation can damage threads, break the rod, and create hazards for both the drill rig and the operator.

Operators also maintain a logsheet to track the drill count (number of rods). By measuring the chuck’s linear feed/stroke, the system can automatically estimate rod count and continuously infer drill depth, reducing manual logging effort and improving traceability during drilling.
